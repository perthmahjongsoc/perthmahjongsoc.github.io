Guide

This (incomprehensive) reference guide is based on Hong Kong gameplay.

There is no such thing as an official version of mahjong. The game should be played according to player consensus.

    Three consecutive tiles of the same numbered suit.

    • When completed by revealing after a discard from the previous player, this is called 上 sheung (going up, or onboarding).
  • 刻子 = Triplet

    Three of the same tile.

    E.g. , , , .

    • When completed by revealing after a discard from another player, this is called 碰 pʻung (bumping).
  • 槓子 = Quadruplet

    Four of the same tile, called a 槓 kong.

    E.g. , , , .

    • When completed by revealing a completely concealed set of four, this is called 暗槓 am kong (concealed kong).

      A concealed kong cannot be robbed for a win, except when the win is a 十三幺 (Thirteen Littles).

    • When completed by revealing after a discard from another player, or by appending to an existing 碰 pʻung, this is called 明槓 ming kong (visible kong).

      A visible kong can be robbed for a win.

Winning

The conventional threshold for a hand to be winning is a minimum of three faan (番, or points).

The conventional maximum for scoring is an upper limit of thirteen faan, quite often reduced to an upper limit of eight faan.

Regular hands

Bonuses

  • +1 faan 門風 = Gate Wind [seat wind]

    A (triplet or quadruplet) meld in a wind tile matching the player's position. Not counted for 小四喜／大四喜 (Lesser Three Joys / Greater Three Joys).

    E.g. for the player to the right of the dealer.

  • +1 faan 圈風 = Round Wind [table wind]

    A (triplet or quadruplet) meld in a wind tile matching prevailing table wind. Not counted for 小四喜／大四喜 (Lesser Three Joys / Greater Three Joys).

    E.g. when West is the prevailing wind.

  • +1 faan [dragon meld]

    A (triplet or quadruplet) meld in a dragon tile. Not counted for 小三元／大三元 (Lesser Three Duxes / Greater Three Duxes).

    E.g. .

  • +1 faan 槓 = Kong (optional)

    A quadruplet meld. (Omitting this bonus may lead to more strategic play, in the form of refusing a kong to keep an in-hand triple concealed.)

    E.g. .

  • +1 faan 自摸 = Self-Drawn

    Winning tile is drawn from the wall, not discarded.

  • +1 faan 無花 = Without Flowers

    No flowers (in games played with flowers).

  • +1 faan 正花 = Correct Flower

    A flower matching the player's position (in games played with flowers). Not counted for 一臺花 (A Table of Flowers).

    E.g. for the player opposite the dealer.

  • +1 faan 門前清 = Gate-Front Clear

    No revealed melds prior to win.

  • +1 faan 搶槓 = Robbed Kong

    Winning tile is revealed when completing a visible kong (or even a concealed kong if the win is 十三幺 (Thirteen Littles)).

  • +1 faan 槓上自摸 = Self-Drawn Atop Kong

    Winning tile is drawn as the compensating tile after a kong (accumulates with consecutive kongs).

  • +1 faan 海底撈月 = Sea-Bottom Scooped Moon

    Winning tile is drawn as the final tile of the wall.

  • +2 faan 一臺花 = A Table of Flowers

    Either four flora or four seasons (in games played with flowers).

  • 13 faan = Maximum 天糊 = Heavenly Win

    Dealer wins with initial hand.

  • 13 faan = Maximum 地糊 = Earthly Win

    Non-dealer wins on dealer's first discard.

Scoring

The Perth Mahjong Society uses the following options:

  • 五一 = Five & One (a base scoring unit of 1)
  • 八番 = Eight faan cap
  • 全銃 = Full Responsibility (discarding player bears all losses)
  • 半辣上 = Half-Spicy Rise (multiplier is arithmetic-mean interpolated after 4 faan)

To score a finished game:

  1. If the game is drawn, scores do not change.
  2. Convert the number of faan to a portion:
    Number of faan Portion
    3 8
    4 16
    5 24
    6 32
    7 48
    8 64
  3. If the game ended with:
    1. 自摸 = self-draw:
      • The winner wins 3 portions.
      • Each other player loses 1 portion.
    2. 打出 = discard:
      • The winner wins 2 portions.
      • The player at fault loses 2 portions.
    3. 包自摸 = guaranteed self-draw:
      • The winner wins 3 portions.
      • The guarantor (the player who supplied enough favourable tiles) loses 3 portions.

For other cases, see the Details section of Conway's mahjong-scorer readme.
